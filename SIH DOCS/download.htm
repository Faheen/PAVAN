“Air pollution 5th largest killer in India”

I came across this interesting picture of an article that was posted about two years ago. It relates quite well to my previous post about air pollution in India. It is interesting to compare the two because the following article was written two years ago however the study in my previous post was very recent. You can see that although the problem is evident, there is not much being done to solve it.

Air pollution 5th largest killing in India

There are a few points and stats that can be pulled out of this article.

  • data has shown that air pollution is resulting in a number of deaths and loss of healthy years of life over time
  • the article says that there are 1 lakh (100,000) new lung cancer cases; lung cancer among non-smokers has shown a 60% increase caused by air pollution
  • women are suffering from indoor air pollution because of biomass in their cooking

India has such a large population and culturally are very set in their ways. That’s why I find it extremely hard to implement policies that can reduce air pollution and maintain a better air quality. It is important for the government to stress the effects on one’s health.

What are your thoughts? 🙂
